Lorena, São Paulo

Lorena is a city with about 90,000 residents, located in São Paulo state, Brazil. Lorena is also home to universities, a growing industrial sector, a rising number of places for physical activities, along with a rich historical heritage.

Universities

The main university in Lorena is USP (University of São Paulo), with its Lorena campus, the School of Engineering of Lorena (EEL). In addition to USP, there are also other institutions such as Unisal, Unifatea, Serra Dourada, and several more.

Industry

Lorena's industrial sector is growing, with companies like Yakult S/A, Mantiqueira, Orica Brasil Ltda, Apolo Tubulars S/A, Perfilor S/A, and AKG do Brasil contributing to the city's economic development. The sector continues to expand, attracting new businesses and offering job opportunities.

Health

Another growing trend in Lorena is the focus on health, with many gyms opening to meet the high demand, along with physical activities like running, dancing, and cycling. Cycling is popular due to the city's flat terrain, and it has even been called the 'City of Bicycles'.

Heritage

Lorena has a rich culture, with architecture, religion, and history standing out. A key landmark is the Casa da Cultura, in the historic Casarão do Conde Moreira Lima. The city was the birthplace of many counts and barons, shaped by coffee plantations that greatly influenced its development.

"I have always resided in this city, which I cherish as my second mother, and here I shall die."

-Count of Moreira Lima.
